# Highway Risk Profiles

Brazil's federal highway network spans over 65,000 km across all 26 states and the Federal District. Certain highways consistently rank among the most dangerous in terms of absolute accident counts and fatality rates.

## Most Dangerous Highways

### BR-101

The BR-101 runs along nearly the entire Brazilian coastline, from Rio Grande do Norte in the northeast to Rio Grande do Sul in the south. It is the highway with the **highest absolute number of accidents** due to its length and heavy traffic volume. It passes through major metropolitan areas including Recife, Salvador, Vitória, Rio de Janeiro, and Florianópolis. Sections in Santa Catarina and Bahia are particularly dangerous due to a mix of heavy truck traffic and tourist vehicles, especially during holiday periods.

### BR-116

The BR-116 is one of Brazil's most important and **deadliest highways by fatality rate**. Running from Fortaleza in the northeast to Jaguarão on the Uruguayan border, it traverses mountainous terrain in several stretches. The segment known as "Rodovia da Morte" (Highway of Death) in the Serra do Rio do Rastro region of Santa Catarina is notorious for its steep grades and sharp curves. The section between São Paulo and Curitiba through the Serra do Cafezal is similarly treacherous, with fog, rain, and steep descents contributing to frequent serious accidents.

### BR-381 (Rodovia Fernão Dias)

Connecting São Paulo to Belo Horizonte through the states of SP and MG, the BR-381 carries enormous commercial traffic between Brazil's two largest economic centers. Much of the highway is single-carriageway with heavy truck traffic, creating dangerous overtaking situations. The mountainous terrain between southern Minas Gerais and northern São Paulo produces frequent fog and reduced visibility conditions.

### BR-040

The BR-040 connects Rio de Janeiro to Brasília, passing through Minas Gerais. The descent from the Serra de Petrópolis near Rio de Janeiro and the stretch through central Minas Gerais are known accident hotspots. The highway serves as a critical logistics corridor, with significant truck traffic contributing to the risk profile.

### BR-153 (Transbrasiliana)

Running from Pará in the north to Rio Grande do Sul in the south, the BR-153 crosses through Goiás, Minas Gerais, São Paulo, Paraná, and Santa Catarina. Long straight stretches encourage excessive speed, and the highway's role as a major freight corridor means heavy truck presence throughout. Sections in Goiás and northern São Paulo are particularly dangerous.

## Common Risk Factors

The highways with the highest fatality rates share several characteristics: long stretches of single-carriageway road (pista simples), mountainous terrain producing curves and grades, heavy commercial vehicle traffic, and sections passing through small towns where speed transitions create conflicts between through traffic and local traffic. Duplication (widening to dual carriageway) projects have shown significant safety improvements where completed, but many dangerous stretches remain single-carriageway.
