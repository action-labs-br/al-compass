# Accident Causes — PRF Taxonomy

The Brazilian Federal Highway Police (PRF) classifies accident causes into standardized categories when recording incidents. Understanding these categories and their relationship to fatality rates is essential for risk analysis.

## Most Common Causes

The following are the most frequently reported causes of accidents on Brazilian federal highways, along with their approximate share of total incidents and associated fatality rates:

- **Reação tardia ou ineficiente do condutor** (~18% of accidents) — The single most common cause. Refers to delayed or ineffective driver reaction to changing road conditions. Fatality rate: ~4%.
- **Falta de atenção** (~16%) — Driver inattention or distraction. Encompasses phone use, drowsiness, and general lack of focus. Fatality rate: ~3.5%.
- **Não guardar distância de segurança** (~12%) — Failure to maintain a safe following distance, leading primarily to rear-end collisions. Fatality rate: ~1.5%.
- **Velocidade incompatível** (~8%) — Driving at speeds incompatible with road conditions, weather, or traffic. Fatality rate: ~8%, one of the highest among common causes.
- **Desobediência à sinalização** (~5%) — Ignoring traffic signs, signals, or road markings. Fatality rate: ~5%.
- **Ingestão de álcool** (~4%) — Drunk driving. Despite representing a smaller share of incidents, fatality rate is approximately ~10%, making it one of the deadliest cause categories.
- **Defeito mecânico em veículo** (~3%) — Mechanical failure including brake failure, tire blowouts, and steering malfunctions. Fatality rate: ~5%.
- **Dormindo** (~2%) — Driver falling asleep at the wheel. Fatality rate: ~12%, the highest among all cause categories due to complete loss of vehicle control.
- **Ultrapassagem indevida** (~3%) — Improper overtaking, particularly on single-carriageway roads. Fatality rate: ~9%, as head-on collisions are common.
- **Animais na pista** (~2%) — Animals on the roadway, more common in rural stretches. Fatality rate: ~3%.

## Key Patterns

Causes with the highest fatality rates are not necessarily the most common. Falling asleep at the wheel, drunk driving, and improper overtaking account for a relatively small share of total accidents but produce disproportionately fatal outcomes. Conversely, failure to maintain safe distance is very common but rarely fatal, as it typically results in low-speed rear-end collisions.

The PRF has noted that cause classification can be subjective, as officers determine the probable cause based on scene evidence and witness statements. Some categories overlap — for instance, "falta de atenção" and "reação tardia" can describe similar driver behavior patterns.

Nighttime accidents show higher rates of alcohol-related and drowsiness-related causes, while daytime accidents are more associated with distraction and failure to maintain distance. Weekend nights, particularly between Friday evening and Sunday morning, concentrate the highest rates of alcohol-involved incidents.
