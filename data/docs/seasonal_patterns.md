# Seasonal Patterns in Highway Accidents

Accident frequency and severity on Brazilian federal highways follow distinct temporal patterns driven by holidays, weather, and traffic volume fluctuations.

## Holiday Periods

The most dangerous periods on Brazilian highways coincide with major holidays when traffic volume spikes dramatically:

- **Christmas and New Year (Operação Rodovida):** The PRF launches its annual Operação Rodovida campaign from mid-December through Carnival. This period concentrates some of the highest daily accident counts, with families traveling long distances between cities. The combination of increased traffic volume, driver fatigue on long trips, and alcohol consumption at celebrations drives both accident frequency and severity upward.
- **Carnival (February/March):** A four-day national holiday that generates massive intercity travel. Coastal highways (particularly BR-101) see enormous traffic increases. Alcohol-related accidents spike significantly during this period.
- **Easter:** Another major travel holiday, typically with a shorter travel window that concentrates traffic into fewer days. Friday and Monday are peak travel days with elevated accident rates.
- **Independence Day (September 7)** and **Republic Day (November 15)** create extended weekends that produce notable traffic volume increases.

## Weekend vs. Weekday Patterns

Weekdays and weekends show markedly different accident profiles. Weekday accidents are concentrated during commuting hours (7-9 AM and 5-7 PM) and are more common in urban segments. Weekend accidents are more dispersed throughout the day, with a notable concentration on Saturday and Sunday nights (10 PM - 3 AM) associated with leisure activities and alcohol consumption.

Critically, **weekend accidents have higher fatality rates** than weekday accidents. This is attributed to higher speeds on less congested roads, greater alcohol involvement, and more nighttime driving.

## Weather and Seasonal Effects

Brazil's rainy season (roughly October through March in the southeast and south) correlates with increased accident rates. Rain reduces visibility and tire grip, particularly dangerous on highways with poor drainage or worn surfaces.

**Fog** is a significant seasonal hazard in the southern highlands. The Serra Gaúcha (Rio Grande do Sul), Serra Catarinense (Santa Catarina), and highland stretches of Paraná experience frequent dense fog during autumn and winter months (May through August). These conditions are particularly dangerous on highways like BR-116 and BR-376, where visibility can drop to near zero.

## Time of Day

Rush hour peaks (7-8 AM and 5-6 PM) show the highest accident frequency, but nighttime accidents (10 PM - 5 AM) have significantly higher fatality rates. Reduced visibility, driver fatigue, and alcohol involvement all contribute to more severe nighttime outcomes. Dawn and dusk transitions also present elevated risk due to glare and visibility changes.
